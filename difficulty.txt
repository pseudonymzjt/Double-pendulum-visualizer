# Difficulties Encountered — Chaotic Art: Double Pendulum Visualizer

> A chronological record of bugs, dead ends, and resolutions encountered during development. Eighteen issues are documented, spanning the Verlet prototype through the final performance optimization pass.

---

## 1. Verlet Gravity Too Weak — The Frozen Pendulum

**Stage**: Stage 7 (Verlet prototype)
**Severity**: Medium
**Symptom**: The pendulum appeared frozen.

**Analysis**: Verlet gravity is computed as `y += vy + G * pxPerUnit * h²`. With `G = 9.81`, `pxPerUnit ≈ 60`, and `h = 1/240 s`, the displacement per sub-step was approximately **0.022 pixels**. At four sub-steps per frame, this yielded ~0.09 px per frame — visually imperceptible. The root cause was a unit-scale mismatch: Verlet applies gravity directly to pixel coordinates, whereas angle-based RK4 produces angular acceleration that scales meaningfully with rod length.

**Resolution**: A scaling factor `VERLET_G_SCALE = 8` was introduced as a temporary measure. The definitive solution — replacing Verlet with exact Lagrangian mechanics — was implemented in Stage 8.

**Lesson**: In Verlet integration, the magnitude of `G * pxPerUnit * h²` must be verified before investigating other potential causes of apparent system inactivity.

## 2. Stale Trails on Joint Modification

**Stage**: Stage 7 (Verlet prototype)
**Severity**: Low
**Symptom**: Visual glitch; console error on joint addition.

**Analysis**: `addJoint` pushed new entries to `particles[]` and `constraints[]` but omitted `trails[]`. `removeJoint` exhibited the inverse omission. The parallel array design (`particles[]`, `constraints[]`, `trails[]`) required atomic updates across all three arrays, which was not enforced.

**Resolution**: Added `p.trails.push([])` to `addJoint` and `p.trails.pop()` to `removeJoint`. A full trail clear was also added to `resizeCanvas`.

**Lesson**: Parallel arrays constitute an anti-pattern. Encapsulating related data into a single object eliminates an entire class of synchronization bugs.

## 3. Verlet Numerical Damping — Loss of Chaos

**Stage**: Stage 7→8 (Physics engine rewrite)
**Severity**: Critical
**Symptom**: The pendulum exhibited regular, predictable motion; no chaotic divergence was observed.

**Analysis**: Three compounding factors:
1. **Constraint iteration overhead**: 4 sub-steps × 10 iterations = 40 position corrections per frame, each bending kinetic energy into positional correction and dissipating energy.
2. **Missing nonlinear coupling**: Verlet treats each particle independently; it lacks the Coriolis-like coupling terms (`ω₁² sin(θ₁−θ₂)`) that drive sensitive dependence on initial conditions.
3. **Double friction on inner bobs**: The 50/50 constraint correction split doubles the effective damping on joints connected to two constraints.

Energy monitoring confirmed total energy dropping from ~30 J to <10 J within 5 seconds.

**Resolution**: The entire physics engine was rewritten using exact Lagrangian mechanics with RK4 integration, Cholesky decomposition, and an N×N mass matrix. Energy drift decreased from tens of percent to **0.0000%**.

**Lesson**: Constraint-based physics introduces mathematically opaque implicit damping. For chaotic systems where energy conservation is critical, Lagrangian mechanics is not merely preferable — it is essential.

## 4. N-Link Locking — The Rigid-Body Bug

**Stage**: Stage 8 (Lagrangian rewrite)
**Severity**: Critical
**Symptom**: For N > 2, bobs moved in perfect lockstep; the last two bobs behaved as a single rigid body.

**Analysis**: Two defects shared a common root cause:
1. `computeParticlePositions` placed bob 3 at angle θ₂ (inheriting bob 2's angle) rather than assigning it an independent θ₃.
2. The solver was hardcoded as a 2×2 system with no equations for θ₃, θ₄, etc.

**Resolution**: Replaced the hardcoded 2×2 solver with a general N-pendulum Lagrangian solver building an N×N mass matrix. Each link now maintains an independent angle in `thetas[]`.

**Lesson**: Generalization from N=2 to N>2 requires an N-parameter architecture from the outset. Post-hoc additions of degrees of freedom produce coupling artifacts that are nearly impossible to diagnose visually.

## 5. Right-Click Deselecting the Context Menu

**Stage**: Stage 8 (Interaction polish)
**Severity**: Medium
**Symptom**: Right-clicking on the canvas caused the contextual menu to disappear.

**Analysis**: The `canvasB` mousedown handler did not filter `e.button`. JavaScript click events fire for all mouse buttons; `selectPendulum(null)` was invoked on any click, including right-clicks (`e.button === 2`).

**Resolution**: Added `if (e.button !== 0) return;` at the handler entry point, and `e.preventDefault()` on the `contextmenu` event to suppress the browser's native menu.

**Lesson**: Canvas event handlers should always guard against non-left-click events. `e.button !== 0` should be a standard template for all mousedown handlers.

## 6. Energy Conservation — Sign Error in Potential Energy

**Stage**: Phase 8 (Metrics implementation)
**Severity**: High
**Symptom**: 341% energy drift; total energy oscillated between −29 J and +113 J.

**Analysis**: The potential energy formula was `PE = g * Σᵢ (N−i) * lᵢ * cos(θᵢ)`, which is intuitive in canvas coordinates (y-axis downward) but inconsistent with the physics convention (y-axis upward). The Lagrangian equations require `V = −g * Σᵢ (N−i) * lᵢ * cos(θᵢ)`. The missing negation caused KE and PE to oscillate in phase rather than in antiphase.

**Resolution**: Changed `PE = g * sum` to `PE = -g * sum`.

**Lesson**: Coordinate system conventions are fundamental in physics simulation. Every formula referencing vertical direction must be audited for sign consistency between canvas and physics conventions.

## 7. Blank Plot Canvas — Missing Argument in Getter

**Stage**: Phase 8 (Metrics rendering)
**Severity**: Medium
**Symptom**: Both energy and phase plots rendered as blank black canvases with no console errors.

**Analysis**: `drawSolidLine` invoked `getX(data[i])` with a single argument, but the energy plot's getter signature was `(_, i) => i`, requiring the index as a second argument. The missing argument produced `undefined`, which `mapX` converted to `NaN`. Canvas `lineTo` silently ignores `NaN` coordinates. The phase portrait's getter (`d => d.thetas[0]`) used only the first argument and did not trigger the bug, which misdirected debugging toward data collection rather than rendering.

**Resolution**: Changed `getX(data[i])` to `getX(data[i], i)`.

**Lesson**: JavaScript's flexible argument handling is convenient but creates silent errors when factory functions pass arguments through. APIs with signature `(value, index)` must always receive both arguments.

## 8. Multiple Pendulum Metrics — Global Array Coupling

**Stage**: Phase 8 (Multi-pendulum support)
**Severity**: High
**Symptom**: Plots displayed incorrect data when switching between pendulums.

**Analysis**: A single global `metricsData` array was populated by `collectMetrics()` based on the currently selected pendulum. Switching selection interleaved data from multiple pendulums with no mechanism to distinguish them. Newly spawned pendulums had no data because they had not yet been selected.

**Resolution**: Each pendulum now owns an independent `metrics[]` array. `collectMetrics()` iterates all visible pendulums, and plot renderers read from the selected pendulum's buffer.

**Lesson**: When data is per-entity, storage must be per-entity. Global buffers create hidden coupling between data collection and rendering that manifests only under specific interaction sequences.

## 9. Energy Plot X-Axis Stagnation

**Stage**: Phase 8 (Metrics refinements)
**Severity**: Medium
**Symptom**: X-axis ticks froze at 299 after the 300-point ring buffer filled.

**Analysis**: The X getter used array index `(_, i) => i`, and `xRange.max = data.length - 1`. In a ring buffer, `data.length` is fixed at capacity once full, decoupling array length from time progression.

**Resolution**: Introduced a monotonic `globalMetricsStep` counter. Each metrics entry stores its step number; the energy plot uses `d => d.step` as the X getter, deriving range from `first.step` and `last.step`.

**Lesson**: Ring buffers decouple data count from time progression. `array.length` is not a proxy for time. Rolling-window visualizations require an external monotonic sequence number for the X axis.

---

## 10. Phase Portrait Wrap-Around Artifact

**Stage**: Phase 8 (Metrics refinements)
**Severity**: Medium
**Symptom**: A straight horizontal line spanned the entire phase portrait after multiple rotations.

**Analysis**: θ₁ was normalized to [0°, 360°). When crossing the 0°/360° boundary (e.g., 359° → 1°), consecutive points were 358° apart in plot space despite being 2° apart in angular space. The path drawer connected them with a straight line across the graph.

**Resolution**: In `drawFadingLine`, a wrap threshold of half the data range (π for [0, 2π)) triggers path breaking when `|x₂ − x₁| > threshold`.

**Lesson**: Angular data lives on a circle, not an interval. Any visualization plotting angular quantities must handle periodic boundary conditions explicitly. Data adjacency does not imply visual adjacency under modular arithmetic.

---

## 11. Angle Display Click Interception

**Stage**: Phase 8 (Metrics refinements)
**Severity**: Medium
**Symptom**: The clickable pendulum selector became unresponsive when plot canvases were open or zoomed.

**Analysis**: Plot canvas `click` handlers called `e.stopPropagation()`, blocking event bubbling to the angle display's `click` listener. Zoomed plot boxes at `z-index: 10` physically overlayed the angle display region, and pointer events were intercepted and suppressed.

**Resolution**: Replaced `click` event delegation with `pointerdown` at the document level, which fires immediately and is unaffected by downstream `stopPropagation()`.

**Lesson**: `click` is a composed event vulnerable to `stopPropagation()` at either `mousedown` or `mouseup` stage. `pointerdown` fires immediately and is more reliable for UI that must remain interactive under overlays.

---

## 12. Energy Plot Title Stagnation on Selection Switch

**Stage**: Phase 8 (Metrics refinements)
**Severity**: Low
**Symptom**: The phase portrait title displayed the wrong pendulum ID after switching selection.

**Analysis**: The title update was positioned after an early-return guard (`if (p.metrics.length < 2) return;`). When switching to a newly created pendulum with insufficient metrics data, the guard triggered before the title could update.

**Resolution**: Moved the title update before the data guard.

**Lesson**: Early-return guards should not prevent metadata updates. State-revealing operations (titles, labels, axis ranges) should precede data-dependent checks.

---

## 13. CSS Specificity Conflict — Code Block Rendering

**Stage**: Post-Stage (Wiki reader)
**Severity**: Medium
**Symptom**: Fenced code blocks in the Guide modal rendered as single lines.

**Analysis**: `#help-modal-content code` (specificity 1,0,0,1) applied `white-space: nowrap` to all `<code>` elements, including those inside `<pre>` blocks. The attempted override `.help-readme-body pre code` (specificity 0,2,0,2) was defeated because an ID selector always outranks a class selector regardless of source order.

**Resolution**: Increased selector specificity to `#help-modal-content .help-readme-body pre code` (1,1,2,1).

**Lesson**: CSS specificity is a weight system, not a proximity system. Reordering rules is futile when selectors have different weights. Overrides must match or exceed the target selector's specificity.

## 14. Mobile Touch Bounce (iOS Safari)

**Stage**: Stage 9 (Mobile support)
**Severity**: High
**Symptom**: Dragging bobs was impossible on iOS; the page rubber-banded instead.

**Analysis**: iOS Safari's elastic overscroll intercepts all touch events. The original touch listeners used `{ passive: true }`, which causes the browser to silently ignore `preventDefault()` calls.

**Resolution**: Implemented three-layer defense:
1. **Compositor level**: `touch-action: none` on canvases.
2. **Document level**: `overscroll-behavior: none` on root elements.
3. **JavaScript level**: `{ passive: false }` listeners with conditional `preventDefault()` during active drag.

**Lesson**: Mobile touch handling requires defense-in-depth. CSS `touch-action: none` (compositor thread) is the most reliable layer, but JS-level `passive: false` + `preventDefault()` remains necessary for full control. The layers are complementary, not redundant.

## 15. Blank Canvas at Startup

**Stage**: Stage 9 (Mobile support)
**Severity**: High
**Symptom**: The pendulum was invisible on initial page load.

**Analysis**: Two independent issues:
1. **No synchronous initial paint**: `animate()` queues via `requestAnimationFrame`, which defers the first frame to the next VSync (~16 ms). During this gap, canvases remain transparent. Removing slowMo code eliminated an incidental "extra" draw that had masked this gap.
2. **Unsafe `matchMedia`**: `window.matchMedia` is not guaranteed in all environments (headless browsers, some WebViews). An unguarded call could throw and halt the entire top-level script.

**Resolution**: Added `window.matchMedia` null guard and synchronous `draw()` + `updateAngleDisplay()` calls before `animate()`.

**Lesson**: `requestAnimationFrame` is not instantaneous. Applications requiring immediate visual feedback need a synchronous paint path. Browser API availability should never be assumed.

## 16. GitHub Pages Deployment Crash

**Stage**: Stage 9 (Deployment)
**Severity**: Critical
**Symptom**: Blank dark page on GitHub Pages; `TypeError: Cannot read properties of null`.

**Analysis**: Two compounding issues:
1. **Missing `defer`**: The `<script>` tag lacked `defer`, causing execution before all DOM nodes were parsed — a condition that never occurred locally due to millisecond-fast file loading.
2. **ID mismatch**: `document.getElementById(id).addEventListener()` threw when the element was `null` (either from timing or a renamed ID), halting all subsequent script execution.

**Resolution**: Added `defer` to the `<script>` tag and created an `on(id, event, handler)` safe-binding function that silently skips missing elements.

**Lesson**: Local development hides timing-dependent bugs. `defer` should be the default for any script referencing DOM elements. Safe-binding patterns (`on(id, event, handler)`) prevent `null` method calls, one of the most common production crash causes in JavaScript.

## 17. Cholesky Positive-Definiteness at Extreme Angles

**Stage**: Late optimization
**Severity**: Low (caught by safety net)
**Symptom**: Potential for silent data corruption at extreme angles with high N.

**Analysis**: Mathematically, M is always SPD. However, floating-point rounding at near-singular configurations can produce tiny negative diagonal elements (e.g., −1e−16), causing `Math.sqrt()` to return NaN in `choleskyFactor`.

**Resolution**: Rather than adding solver-level fallback logic, NaN propagation is caught one frame later by `isPendulumInvalid()` / `safeResetPendulum()`, which resets the pendulum to a safe state (135°, zero velocity, cleared trails). Hard caps (`MAX_LINKS = 8`, `MAX_PENDULUMS = 8`) prevent entry into the near-singular region.

**Lesson**: Not every numerical edge case requires a mathematical fix. A system-level safety net is often the cheaper, more robust solution — provided the safety net is reliable and the triggering conditions are rare and self-correcting.

## 18. Float64Array Sync Across Mutation Points

**Stage**: Late optimization
**Severity**: Low (latent bug)
**Symptom**: Potential for the physics solver to read stale link lengths.

**Analysis**: `p.ls_f64` mirrors `p.ls` for GC-free hot-path access. Four mutation points modify `p.ls`: `addJoint`, `removeJoint`, `rebuildChain`, and `resizeCanvas`. The initial implementation missed `resizeCanvas`, which alters `pxPerUnit` and thus all link lengths.

**Resolution**: Added `p.ls_f64.set(p.ls)` at all four mutation points, with a backward-compatibility guard in `rebuildChain`.

**Lesson**: Mirroring mutable state into a typed array creates a synchronization surface requiring meticulous maintenance. A centralized helper function (`syncLsF64`) localizes the sync logic and reduces the risk of future omissions. The root issue is data redundancy — the same information exists in two representations, demanding explicit consistency maintenance.
